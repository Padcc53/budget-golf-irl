Welcome

Weather is such a big factor especially if you have shelled out to play a links course.


As a rule of thumb from October to early March the weather is in the lap of the Gods you could get anything.


From mid March to early October you can hope for the best but in Ireland with weather you always plan for the worst.


The best website to use is ireland's national weather forecasting service which is called Met Eireann and you can find it here


Remember that everyone plays in the same conditions, so no point in getting frustrated by the elements


Planning

How Many In Your Group

A small group four or less gives you standard pricing


A bigger group let's say six or more means for most mid to expensive courses, you can definately try for a cheap rate at off peak hours


A group of twelve or more, the mid price to expensive courses will negociate especially if it's a resort and accomodation is in play as well


The bigger the group you have, the more advantageous is it to book as early as you can.

Where to play and when

Start with the cheap course and then the expensive course and repeat.

Playing the cheap course first gets your game warmed up for the course you are spending more money on.


Mixing in the cheaper courses means people have more leeway to enjoy the night before and I think the multi day trips tend to be more fun this way

Accomodation

If you can stay within 90 Kms, stay in the same place for multiple nights for cheap rates

If you have an early morning start it means being close to the course the night before. Do not tire yourself out for golf by getting up early to travel


You will avoid being stiff after the journey and you can get a good breakfast into you.

Travel

Groups of eight or more should consider a mini bus, the cost divided up means less driving on narrow roads

Otherwise car hire is the way to go. Be careful you do not get scammed by the car hire companies Read this
